BLOG: 8 Tips For Teaching your Child to Read

1.Choose a Comfortable Place to Study

Ensure the study area is safe, well-lit, and comfortable. A pleasant environment will encourage your child to read more pages without feeling uncomfortable.

2. Read books for children

Start reading to your child at an early age, even as young as 2-3 years. Children can memorize the entire story, and even if their speech isn't perfect, you will hear them trying to repeat the details.

3. Make Reading a Routine

Create a daily reading habit. For example, read together after coming home or during quiet moments. This routine will make reading enjoyable and will encourage them to ask for books regularly.

6. Combine reading and writing

Don't rely solely on school or kindergarten to teach. At home, encourage your child to write and draw, starting with letters and their own name. This builds foundational skills in both reading and writing.

7.Paste ABC alphabet on bedroom wall

Decorate their bedroom with ABC charts and pictures. This will help them subconsciously memorize letters while associating learning with their personal space.

8. Take your child to the bookstore and library

Make it a habit to visit bookstores or libraries. Allow your child to explore the environment while you read together. This can create fond memories and develop a lifelong love for books.
